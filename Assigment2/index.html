Major Earthquakes from 1965 - 2016

Earthquakes are the shaking of the earth’s surface caused by a sudden release of energy, creating seismic waves. Earthquakes can be so small humans can’t detect them, so violent they can destroy cities or anything in between. They strength of the quake is measured in magnitude, which is a scale from 1 to 9. A magnitude of 5.3 is a moderate earthquake, while 6.3 is strong. The scale is logarithmic, meaning each 1 unit increase represents a tenfold increase in strength. Magnitude and Depth of Earthquakes

Depth refers to how far below the surface an earthquake occurred, while magnitude is a measure of the maximum movement caused by the earthquake.

This graph explores the relationship between these factors to attempt to find a correlation. By clicking on a continent on the legend, you can focus on just that area. Additionally, the dial below the graph allows you to change the time period displayed.

Significant earthquakes are labelled with the year they occurred – only earthquakes with a magnitude over 7.5 and depth greater than 400km are labelled.
